# First Principles

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the first principles that guide the system.

These principles protect coherence, reduce unnecessary complexity, and keep the project human-centered.

## 2. Human First

The human remains the center.

Technology supports the human.

Technology does not replace human responsibility.

## 3. Experience First

Real experience comes before architecture.

The system starts from lived experience, learning, interviews, problems, plans, and follow-up.

## 4. Understanding Before Building

Do not build before understanding.

Observe first.

Extract patterns.

Understand.

Name.

Formalize.

Build.

Verify.

Share.

## 5. Architecture Before Complexity

Architecture must exist before complexity.

Do not add agents, automation, memory, backend, or production before the foundations are clear.

## 6. Proposal Is Not Execution

Proposal != Execution.

Approve != Execute.

No tool, agent, or assistant executes sensitive actions without human approval.

## 7. Human Approval

Human approval remains mandatory.

The Owner remains responsible for final decisions.

## 8. Holberton Method

One step.

One verification.

One correction.

One improvement.

No skipping fundamentals.

Practice over perfection.

## 9. Coherence Over Speed

Do not move fast into confusion.

Coherence is more important than adding features.

## 10. Tools Have Roles

ChatGPT acts as brain and mentor.

Notion AI acts as secretary and operating system.

Codex acts as controlled builder.

Git and GitHub act as delivery and evidence system.

## 11. Boundaries

No AGI claims.

No artificial consciousness claims.

No autonomous execution.

No surveillance.

No manipulation.

No access to private data without consent.

No guaranteed outcomes.

## 12. Core Pattern

Learn

↓

Understand

↓

Formalize

↓

Build

↓

Verify
